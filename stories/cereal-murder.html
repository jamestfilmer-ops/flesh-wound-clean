Cereal Killer

Story  ·  Loading...

10:05 PM

"Hello."

"Hey, Sam, it's Fred. Listen, Barnie and I were out on a call and… well, I think you should come take a look."

"Okay. How serious is it, Fred?"

"Just get here and see."

10:28 PM

"I'll be damned. Same M.O. and everything?"

"To the letter."

"So we are looking for a detailed copycat. A —"

"No Sir. Not a copycat. The gold, the charms between the teeth, even the symbols painted on the wall… No, these are details we did not share with the public. Only people in our crew would know 'em."

"Well it can't be L.C. He's been in prison for going on twenty years now."

"Yes, but not in solitary. His communication privileges were granted last year. Ten years good behavior."

"So, what're you saying? We have a partner?"

"It was an idea in the original killings – that he was working with someone; we just never had enough evidence to legitimize the theory."

"Why now, though? Why ten years later?"

"Perhaps he was the subordinate, the sidekick, waiting for his big moment? To build up the courage? To show he didn't need L.C.'s guidance?"

"Maybe. Let me make a call. I want to meet with him."

12:15 AM

"L.C., how you been?"

"Can you explain why I was roused from my beautiful cell? Do cops not sleep?"

"We just want to ask you a few questions."

"Open your eyes, Lieutenant. You got me, the cuffs are on."

"Greeny, you —"

"Don't call me that."

"What? Greeny?"

"Yes. I'm already in cuffs, no need to insult me."

"You killed twenty people; you lost all ounce of my respect. Like it or not, I still control you. And you're going to keep talking or not see sunlight for a year."

"Fine. Let's get to talkin'!"

"Did you have a partner?"

"…A partner?"

"A partner."

"Can't say I understand."

"It's pretty damn simple. Did you have a partner?"

"You think that dead sheriff messed up that badly? Put me away and leave a loose end like that?"

"Tonight, three men were left dead in an apartment on the South side. On the walls the four-leaf clover was painted in their blood. All three of them had gold coins shoved in their mouth."

"I didn't have a partner."

"So which is it, then? You been playin' hooky or do we have a copycat?"

"That's not what I said."

"What'd you say then?"

"I did not have a partner. I had a … follower. Some damn kid."

"A kid?"

"A kid. This damn reject with a tick. He used to follow me around until I used to threaten to kill him one night at Apple and Cinnamon's bar."

"Before you killed Buzz Bee."

"Yeah."

"What's he look like?"

"Man. What's in it for me?"

"Three men are dead, Charms. We aren't negotiating."

"Seems like I have a description and you have the power to make this box bearable."

"Tell you what. Give me a name and I'll see what I can do getting you some more yard time."

"I want off death row."

"No."

"Have fun, Sheriff."

"Fine. I will vouch to the court for you. Just give me a name."

"Sonny. But the streets they call him Cuckoo."

"Cuckoo?"

"Cuckoo."

· · ·

"Hey Sam."

"Yeah?"

"The guys that were killed. Their names?"

"What does that matter?"

"Makes the difference between if I feel bad or not. Three dead peddlers or pervs versus three family men, you know?"

"…"

"So?"

"The names were Snap, Crackle, and Pop. They were college roommates. Straight A's. Good boys."

"Interesting."

"These weren't peddlers or pervs, L."

"Hey Sam. Before you go."

"Yeah?"

"When I had my deal with C in the alley, I saw his car. License plate said P-U-F-F."

"PUFF?"

"As in cocoa puffs."
