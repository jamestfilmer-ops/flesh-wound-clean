The Artist

Story  ·  Loading...

"If the highest aim of a captain is to preserve his ship, he would keep it in port forever." — Thomas Aquinas
"A noble craft, but somehow a most melancholy! All noble things are touched with that." — Herman Melville

"Oh, High Almighty! What a tempest you've brought forth!" Captain Hugo proclaimed, shadowed by an evolving creature — no, a behemoth — in the sky above. Bolts of pain engulfed mens hands as they hurled the various woven cords amidst the rain. Harrowing winds harpooned droplets at their skin and eyes like a frozen death. Blue, turquoise, green, grey, black was the ocean beneath; swirls of thrashing waters clamored against the hull of The Artist, echoing into the night.

Lightning hurled silhouettes of beasts below the water's surface, like spirits from the deep aching for dominion of the depths. Thunder shot into their hearts and shook their teeth — war drums of unseen armies. In the near distance, Louis heard the chaotic chorus of his fellow shipmates. It seemed to evolve into a growling laughter; one unified cackle. The wood beneath his feet made an ominous creaking sound, then he felt her push against him. She's lifting, he thought.

And so she was.

One swell amassed ahead of her bow, a tower of water beyond belief. Her bow bent into the trough, hurled down the slope, screaming amidst the racket of wind and rain. Louis weaved himself into the rigging and heard Captain Hugo's blasting curses diminish to a distant whisper. His arms and legs, now interlocked to the icy cables, were rigid and quaking. Drums. Crashing. Beasts. Lightning. Drums. Crashing. Beasts. Lightning. Heaven had traded tranquility for violence, and its executioner now stood above the foremast with vile hatred.

Eddie and Godfrey — the feeble cook and wanting gunsmith — abandoned their positions on the gunwales. Exuding fear and desperation, they dashed towards Louis at the stern's rigging. He watched their faces comprehend the madness; their eyes lift upon the horrific realization — they were too late. When The Artist punched the beast at her lowest point, they vanished into the evil spirit's jowls. The wave plummeted on deck in a chaotic, senseless act of rage.

"Lies, they are, of nature's kindness. An unfolded villain upon the deep."

As the ship rose from the pit, Louis closed his eyes. The harmony of wind seemed to silence for just a moment. A brief blaze of relief warmed his skin. In his brief banishment from madness, he heard a faint rally behind him. "Lies, they are, of nature's kindness. An unfolded villain upon the deep." It was Hugo, singing the old bar chant in a state of psychosis at the helm. "But who brings forth the wily waves! Not me! Not I! I just see graves!" Churning water continued to seep into the walls of her frame. Cannons and barrels sloshed across the deck.

"Cast her out, pay her dues; the rum shall die and so will you! Whist me up unto the veil and crash me down, below the sail!" Hugo cried.

Louis turned to look towards heaven, for restitution, for restraint. Lightning whispered in the distance; in the faint light of its crackling, he thought he saw something. Beyond the demons in the deep, and the dagger-sharp waves, breathed a shadow — a shore, or a cliff.

Then, it vanished.

The voice was softer now, "And shall the tyrants flip their swords, so all the men remain aboard." This final line gripped for Louis as he was pulled into the leviathan.
