A Plan for the Greatest Beer Run of All Time

May 4, 2025  ·  Loading...

In the olden days, explorers would seek investments from prosperous businessmen and governments to finance their grand adventures. Trips to the Poles and journeys to discover new trade routes were incredibly costly, and the only way to pay for food, supplies, ships, and deckhands was to promise treasure, media tours, book deals, and that your country could claim your discoveries to be its own.

Now consider the current day — there is nothing left to explore except the depths of the sea and the vastness of space, which are doable, but costly and limited by the laws of physics. Instead, we have it so plentiful that we must make the same journeys, but in stylized and more difficult fashions. We must not journey round and find a way across Niagara Falls, we must tightrope across it. We have already exited and reentered the Earth's atmosphere, but what if we free fall from it just to see if we can? We have already gone down the road to share a drink with some friends, but what if we circumnavigated the globe for a few pints, instead? And this is where the incredibly gluttonous, naive, and childish parts of me come alive. Perhaps one day we can find a generous benefactor to fund a fortnight of memories — or we can just save our money.

Here is the "vacation" that most people I come across say sounds miserable, although I know for a fact a few of you reading are up for the task.

· · ·

1. North America — My favorite pick is The Bell in Hand Tavern, which is America's oldest, opening its doors in 1795 in Boston, MA. It's also an excuse to stop at Fenway Park. Some other options include The White Horse Tavern (Newport, RI), built in 1673, which wreaks of old colonial history; La Mela (NYC, NY), just an excuse to visit New York's Little Italy; and McGillin's Olde Ale House (Philadelphia, PA), opening in 1865 with the interior you would expect of an Irish pub.

2. Europe — This one is difficult for me. The Eagle and Child wins out. As a fan of C.S. Lewis, visiting the meeting place of the Inklings in St. Giles, Oxford would be special. However, there are dozens of good choices which also include U Fleků (Prague, Czech Republic), established in 1499 and serving ale for over 500 years; The Captain Kidd (London, England), not far from where infamous pirates such as William Kidd were hanged; and Sean's Bar (Athlone, Ireland), recognized as one of the oldest pubs in the world, opening in the year 900 A.D. Just imagine a glass of Guinness here.

3. Africa — My choice is Khufu's, a rooftop dining spot overlooking the pyramids in Cairo, Egypt. Named after Pharaoh Khufu, who commissioned the pyramids around 2580 BC, this is less of a dive bar as the ones listed above, but no doubt has plenty to offer. Other options include Sani Mountain Lodge (Mokhotlong, Lesotho), the highest pub on the continent at 2,874m above sea level, offering a beautiful hike through the South African countryside; and Rooftops at Z Hotel (Nungwi Beach, Zanzibar), right on the ocean front. You can't go wrong.

4. Asia — MO Bar in Singapore is swankier than some of the others but looks to offer a rooftop experience next to a major airport that's too good to pass up. Other options include Sky Bar at Lebua (Bangkok, Thailand), another skyline party spot; and Good Ol Tennessee Bar (Tokyo, Japan), operated by Yoshitoshi Momoki, who graduated from UT in 1993. I'd likely get kicked out once I brought up Zach Edey.

5. Oceania — Lord Nelson's, the oldest licensed pub in Sydney, combines my desire to visit Australia with my love for nautical history. Other options include The Green Dragon Inn (Hobbiton, New Zealand) — this one is aimed directly at my friend Zuri, who if he's reading this, come on man, let's get this thing planned — and Squire's Landing (Sydney, Australia), with a clear shot of the Opera House.

6. Antarctica — I'm not sure if we can even legally do this one, but Vernadsky Research Base is home to the southernmost bar in the world and is owned and operated by Ukraine. This is the part of the trip that Rebecca would skip — she would not like the cold. The isolated European research base is accessible, but you have to be part of an Antarctic cruise.

7. South America — For fans of the movie Butch Cassidy & the Sundance Kid, Museo Bar La Legal in Cholila, Argentina, in the heart of Patagonia, is not far from the site where the duo lived for years while on the run. Other options include Floreria Atlantico (Buenos Aires), named one of the World's 50 Best Bars in 2024 and hidden in a flower shop; and Café Tortoni (Buenos Aires), which has hosted the likes of Albert Einstein, Francis Ford Coppola, and Katy Perry — basically a mandatory stop if you're going to be in Argentina.

· · ·
Explore and enjoy in your life with friends and cherish those memories.

Fly back home, and check yourself in to AA — because you'll have so many American Airlines points it won't even be funny. Oh… I guess yeah, the other AA would make sense there.

Anyways. I asked AI how much this would all cost, flying economy and all. Assuming we stay at a cheap Airbnb or hotel one night in each location, and factoring in meals, using my top choices for each, it's about $8,500 for flights and lodging. Though, if you take out the mandatory Antarctic cruise, it lowers it significantly to $3–4k.

I know money is tight for a lot of folks. Most of my friends are still in their early twenties and a multi-continental bar crawl is not in the cards — it isn't for Rebecca and I, that's for certain. Regardless, the underlying goal that drives me to write this stays the same: explore and enjoy in your life with friends and cherish those memories.

Lastly, what's a little credit card debt? (For legal reasons, this is a joke.)
