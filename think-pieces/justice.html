Justice

2022  ·  Loading...

I have written a poem in hopes of conveying the totality of my ideas on Justice. If I forget to mention or do not feel qualified to cover an item in the following essay, hopefully the poem might fill in the gaps.

The will held captive,
Scales obeyed,
Limits nearby,
Hearts imprisoned.

Fair and good,
The soul can't cease
To bear thy journey
Through Death's grip.

Guilt a spear
Innocence betrayed?
Shall it be at will
That the will's dismayed?

A power not fit,
A trust withheld,
Mercy, must she reign,
And Justice strikes her down.

· · ·

'An eye for an eye' is all well and good until a nation is blind and living in darkness. At some point, there must be a limitation set into place; and the solution cannot spread the fire. It is on these two sides of the road where people often find themselves. There are those who want strict enforcement of the law, harsh punishments, and little to do with mercy. On the flip side, many advocate for leniency, forgiveness, and 'social care'. You have likely heard of the elephant metaphor; every blind man has a hint of a part of the elephant. 'It's a tree.' 'No, it's a snake.' I believe we have been doing the same thing with justice and the courts.

Justice is an area in which we certainly want there to be a middle ground. The classic image of Lady Justice, blindfolded and holding her scales, comes to mind. I must trust those before me who believed this to be a proper representation of Justice. However, I also propose a new look.

Perhaps Justice is still a blindfolded woman. But I like to think she has a spear in one hand and a heart in the other.

The spear: Discipline. The heart: Mercy. One side of her body, split down the middle from her head to her toes, is silver, solid stone — this is the side that holds the spear. It is unwavering, cold, and immobile. Opposite of the spear is a magnolia tree, with branches spun and twisted into the shape of a female figure. It blooms with compassion and, in a set of twigs comprising a human hand, it holds the heart. Justice is brute force as much as it is mercy and care. God judged and punished, banishing Lucifer to Hell, throwing Adam and Eve from paradise, and all the while knowing He would send Christ to forgive.

Justice is a woman we all know. We have all seen her bad side. The spear she holds is not made of rubber, and she certainly does not hesitate to use it on us and our peers. For some reason, some men have found this power to be rewarding. They attempt to capture and distribute the harsh side of justice; if they succeed, wielding it becomes their favorite pastime.
