Dear Representatives

May 2022  ·  Loading...

I have sent this letter to every individual who represents the state of Indiana. To foster bipartisan work on this issue, I have made no distinction between representatives. Hopefully, this allows for a common ground. Setting all other issues aside, genuinely and with humility, I hope this message fosters the kind of renewal and progress that this nation needs.

For a brief introduction, my name is Isaac Lindell. I am a student at Purdue University and have lived in Indiana my entire life. I am not an activist, an aspiring politician, an intern for any campaign, or a media representative. I have no blue check mark on Twitter, nor do I have a significant enough wealth to pursue any kind of change on my own. I am writing as a concerned citizen; hopefully, this unbiased approach can spearhead a helpful reunion of minds and move us closer to a more unified, intellectual-debate-based society. I love my state. As my representatives, I hope you do as well.

It is time for the part where I express my frustration, not only at the lack of response to the situation, but at the very nature of our Union. We seem hellbent on suffocating the chance for a proper response to situations like Uvalde, TX by heaping piles of propaganda and slogans from lobbyists and political affiliates on top of the matter. Hopefully, the Hoosier state is where we change course, showing the nation it is possible to elect politicians capable of stepping into other peoples' shoes — whether they are Democratic shoes, Republican shoes, or light-up Sketchers your mom bought you to wear to your 2nd grade art class.

As you are all aware, kids were gunned down as if they were protecting Bin Laden in a raid on a school in Uvalde, TX. Current reports estimate 19 children, and 2 teachers, were slaughtered like animals.

I have sent this letter in the hope that someone reads it who has the power to act and the conscience to do so. I do not pretend to have all the answers. I only pretend to be a citizen who is tired of watching the same conversation go nowhere while people bury their children.
