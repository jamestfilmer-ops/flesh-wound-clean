In Pursuit of Good

2022  ·  Loading...

I am no philosopher, though I do enjoy the occasional book or lecture; nor am I an intellectual, but that is not going to stop me from commenting and reviewing my beliefs on the world. Every mind has something to offer, so maybe this is what I can offer in my time on Earth. Is it possible that in a few years, my ideas will be proven irrelevant and tossed into the waste bin? Certainly. But it appears I still have a fair amount of time on this Earth, and I would like to do with it what I can. I am going to take a crack at defining good, and then define how that plays into my life and my motivations for choosing my life choices. Do with it what you will.

In middle school, my humanities teacher had us try and define good. You can imagine we were all pretty confused. "Uh, Mr. Prible, good is defined in the dictionary as 'to be desired or approved of; having the qualities required for an approved role'. Now what?" I cannot remember what I wrote, but yesterday I sat down, trying to work on another section of this book, and thought about what it meant to be good and to do good. We say that all the time: "Do good." George, my old neighbor, used to say, "You're a good man, Charlie Brown." It is so engrained into our vocabulary, often used as the simplest adjective. What does "good" mean?

In my opinion, "good" is the best balance of justice, truth, utilitarianism, and beauty at a given point.

This seems to encompass the basic things that are good. "This is a good sandwich." So it is a sandwich that was created from fair means (you did not steal it from the homeless man down the street), it is a real sandwich (you will not find that the crust is made from clay and nails inside), it satisfies the needs for you and the maker of the sandwich to the maximum degree of utility, and it maximizes the pleasurable feeling in the senses in the way that it looks and smells, so it is a beautiful sandwich.

It was once explained to me that good is what aligns with God's will. This fits that definition as well. "Love your neighbor as yourself." If you are "good" to your neighbor, is this not love? If God is love, then God is good, also. There are some tremendous leaps and pages of deliberation here, but for the sake of the draft, I shall continue.

This also encompasses the grand idea of good, it seems. To live a "good" life, you are just and do not extort or harm anybody, you are honest, not selfish and serve others, and you either create beautiful things or you enjoy the beautiful things that happen to you. To enact good on another person, you treat them fairly, are honest to them, see them as an equal first and act accordingly, and then help others to see beauty. I am keeping it rather simple.

· · ·

I am aware that I am painting with a brush broader than the horizon, so bear with me. The issue is that there are an infinite number of layers to this discussion. I cannot bear to type it all out. I will continue on and look forward to having discussions with my peers and you, the reader, about these topics. I am always open to criticism.
