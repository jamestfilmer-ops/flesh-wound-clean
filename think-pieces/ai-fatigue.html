AI Fatigue

2023  ·  Loading...

My wife and I welcomed our firstborn, Phoebe, a few months ago and, as so many said it would, it has altered just about everything. From our circadian rhythm to how I perceive the news to what music I listen to and what movies we watch — just about everything is different. Truthfully, if all the change in my life was within our apartment walls, it might still take months or even years to discern what's what.

But it's not all within our apartment, is it? By now, there isn't a post, an ad, an email, or a news article free of an association with AI. The room for doubt has flipped, and it's almost easier to assume ChatGPT wrote what you just read than to bet your bottom dollar a human thought it and typed it all out, or took the picture themselves, or wrote that song. In recent months, it's become incredibly clear that the technology in its current state, let alone its more advanced iterations, is set to introduce a level of change that many of us are not prepared for.

This article is intended for those who are fearful or confused like I am. Not just for my role — but for my neighbors.

Nothing new under the sun. That's what the scripture says, and I believe it — but it doesn't make the pace of it any less disorienting when you're holding a newborn at 3 a.m. and trying to figure out what world she's going to grow up in.
