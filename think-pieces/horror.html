Horror

2022  ·  Loading...

"There are horrors beyond life's edge that we do not suspect, and once in a while man's evil prying calls them just within our range." — H.P. Lovecraft

Why must the opposing perception of beauty be Horror? Because it hedges us from the supposed subjectivity of beauty, masking the true villainy of its absence. In any case, the absence of beauty — the soldier that holds off death — results in catastrophe. When defined as ugliness or disgust, the antithesis of beauty masquerades as an opinion rather than a theme. After the fire burns down the forest, only ghosts can inhabit it; the blood rushing down the bride ruins her dress. These are not simply "ugly" or "not beautiful"; they are horrific, indeed. Horror is the best way to describe beauty's absence because that which is farthest from beauty must be closest to death.

The reason we are so genuinely terrified by horror is because we fear death and suffering. It is no coincidence that the horror films, photos, tragedies, and speeches are closely linked to misery. Demons, darkness, torture — these only exist farthest from the sun. The number one way to fight horror is to build beauty wherever you can. If there is a body in the dirt, give it a flower. If the house has succumbed to flames, use the charcoal for art.

The people who have the greatest smiles, provide the most comfort, and sacrifice themselves for the greater good are the ones who have seen the most horror and survived.

This is so clear in our society that it is almost like a law of physics. The best sense of humor arises from the best victory against the evil in our lives. Those who escape, at least. The mind struggles to comprehend the horrors we can inflict on each other; as a result, some minds can never understand the light again. If you remove somebody from the light and thrust them into suffering, they may never trust the light again. After all, wasn't the light safe? Some people learn to trust the light again, and it favors them with a sense of humor. At this point, they can mock the darkness. Others learn to despise the light and feel comfortable in secret.

The irony is this: when you are in the dark, you can see the least amount of danger, even though it surrounds you in hoards. A life in pure despair — Hell, you may say — is where some feel most comfortable.

· · ·

I have always wondered why these constants exist in our universe. Clearly, we did not make them up. If we were created, we were created to see the light; if we evolved, we evolved to see the light just the same — these realities can coexist. Light existed before us, so darkness did as well. But why must these things exist? Would it not be easier for nothing to appear than for something? Nothing is death allowing and something is death defying. I guess by even a grain of sand existing, there is beauty in the world.

Can somebody love evil? It does seem impossible to do so if love is a weapon for good. It is possible, however, to feel comfort in the presence of evil. Comfort is often equated with love, but it does not imply love's existence. There are three types of individuals regarding horror — those that chase it, those that sit and dine within it, and those that run from it. The men that chase horror are either testing themselves or desiring the suffering of others. Life is suffering, but there is a reason for it. Suffering is the flame that mends a man's character into an unyielding blade. Pain is the hammer; unbearable, though it may be, no soldier can fight a battle with a dull iron brick.
