Goodness: A Layman's Interpretation

2022  ·  Loading...

I have rewritten so much of this that it gives me a headache. I can never seem to get any of it right. On the one hand, I feel the need to tell you that I am not a 'qualified' author or anyone of note, and therefore you should take all the following ideas with a grain of salt. On the other, I recognize that sometimes good ideas come from ordinary people. 'Ordinary' would be the best way to describe me.

My desire is this: whether you heed the words in this piece, you strive for goodness, whatever that is. This is an attempt to tackle what "goodness" actually means.

I believe goodness is the optimal balance between Truth, Beauty, Justice, and Sacrifice. These are what I'll call The Pillars. I am a Christian, and thus I recognize the belief that God is good, that He is love. Church words only go so far, however. I wish to leave us with a better understanding of what it means for something to be 'good.'

"Goodness is the optimal balance between Truth, Beauty, Justice, and Sacrifice."

Truth

"Truth is so obscure in these times, and falsehood so established, that, unless we love the truth, we cannot know it." — Blaise Pascal

Truth is the relationship between Reality and belief. If Belief and Reality were human beings, their friendship would be "the truth." The Truth does not thrive when they are far apart and angry at one another. On the other hand, when they are best friends, interweaving their lives, the Truth is relatively strong. We need both because truth "exists" in the physical world of empirical evidence and matter and in the metaphysical sphere.

There is something truthful about Jiminy Cricket. I think truth tugs at us. We know what is true and running from it never seems to go the way we want. But the cricket keeps chirping in our ears until we face it. The conscience, whatever the enigma is, might be our proximity meter to the Truth. A cricket jumps and alerts at the sign of trouble. When it is darkest, cricket is the loudest. It is not hard to "kill" a conscience. It happens gradually and can be like lifting your foot and crushing a cricket — swift and easy.

Attempts to reign in the Truth are like burning the whole farm because you don't like the crickets. Best to let them run free because you will never get them all. There's always one that escapes and keeps making noise.

· · ·

Beauty

"Beauty awakens the soul to act." — Dante Alighieri

Some people sit stranded, wondering where they should go in this life. When you are deep in sorrow, eating from the waste bin, begging for coins, on your death bed — these are the times when you must chase Beauty, an elusive and fascinating creature.

I believe in the power of the beautiful, and I think that if one has life, there is a remnant of Beauty within us all. Beauty is how we measure our proximity to Death. The truly beautiful things stave off Death for an unimaginable length of time — mountainsides, sunsets, paintings, literature, poetry, and humans.

I cannot emphasize enough how important it is for a person to create. If you can create something to which you can attach meaning, you have inevitably given yourself meaning, and thus you are not worthless. Sit on the street, and watch the cars go by. Look at the greenery, architecture, or how the clouds make a smiley face. Find something new in your world.

"Death wants us to sink into the past. Beauty draws on the power of the future, the everlasting, the idea that things outlive us."

Death wants us to sink into the past. Everything is dying, wasting away. He is the shadow that follows you, saying, "Nothing you have ever done has or will mean anything." When you are in that dark void, the cure to his nagging is to focus on Beauty so he does not get the satisfaction of your despair.

The mountains will form somewhere else on another plate as it moves below the Earth. The forests turned to ash to fertilize the soil for new plants, animals, and trees to spawn. Nature is beautiful, and it cannot be destroyed. Beauty fights onward. Death may win a battle here and there, but the war is over. Beauty won.

· · ·

Justice

"The arc of the moral universe is long, but it bends towards justice." — Rev. Martin Luther King Jr.

"An eye for an eye" is all well and good until a nation is blind and living in darkness. Justice is an area in which we certainly want there to be a middle ground. The classic image of Lady Justice, blindfolded and holding her scales, comes to mind. I propose a new look, however — she has a spear in one hand and a heart in another: the Spear, Discipline; and the Heart, Mercy.

One side of her body, split down the middle, is grey, solid stone — this is the side that holds the Spear. It is unwavering, cold, and immobile. Part of Justice is being firm. Otherwise, punishment means nothing. As G.K. Chesterton wrote, "The true soldier fights not because he hates what is in front of him, but because he loves what is behind him."

But Justice does not only require the Spear. It requires the Heart too. Mercy might be the hardest thing for a person to give away. It is a paradox: the most incredible mercy feels the most unjust. If you forgive all, no one is left to judge. Those who have been forgiven forgive others and are less likely to transgress another time. Forgiveness remains the most vital act of love.

· · ·

Sacrifice

The final pillar required in the pursuit of good is Sacrifice — when a person is willing to lose part of themselves for others. This does not necessarily mean laying down one's life in blood, sweat, and tears. Any time a loss is incurred to advance another's pursuit, it is a sacrifice. Time, money, love, life, respect, and power are some things we hold dear and can give to others.

Sacrifice can only be characterized by love put into action. Otherwise, it is nothing more than foolish, naïve ignorance. Sacrifice has a goal — to make something better at the expense of what you could be. This is love at its finest.

Sacrifice cannot be forced; it must be a willing pursuit of a loss — hence, it constitutes love. If you force a man to lose part of himself, you are a thief. Utilitarianism is the natural and self-induced adventure towards utopia; totalitarianism is the deceitful portrayal and means of achieving utopia. One cannot have both.

· · ·

Heaven knows I have not figured it all out. Keep pursuing goodness; it is important now as much as ever.
