Betrayal

2022  ·  Loading...

"Bread of deceit is sweet to a man; but afterwards his mouth shall be filled with gravel." — Proverbs 20:17 KJV

Well, we discussed truth; you may disagree with my definition. I was trying to be novel and introduce a philosophical concept through the perspective of an amateur. That can be done away with from the experts if they desire. What does not change — and this is one of the few hills that I will die on — is the importance of truth. I stand by that portion of the piece. But this book accomplishes nothing if I leave you with my opinions and a hardy slogan. "Tell the truth! It's important!" If only history told us men would listen to such a concept.

Betrayal is the clearest form of evil. It is right there in your face, in the town square, screaming from the rooftops. It is not a wound you can push through. You feel it right away. At least with the remaining of Evil's vices — Pride, Horror, and Injustice — it can be hidden for a little while. After all, those stay in the heart a little longer. Betrayal is a dagger in the hand of a man. And he's running.

Betrayal is impulsive and a lingering sting. Once you notice the truth, it instantly hurts. Anger rises.

I have tried to make it clear that all of these "creatures of the battle" are interconnected. Without Beauty, Sacrifice is harder, and Truth leads to Justice, etc. The same concept appears with Evil, no doubt. Betrayal is the precursor to injustice. See, Injustice is the standing water of pain from Betrayal. It sits there, stupors, growing in potency and grief, saturated in misery. All of it grows stronger with time. Betrayal was the storm that brought the puddle; and Injustice festers when not dealt with. If Betrayal begins the process, we start there.

When a man can trust his fellow soldier, action is done; without trust, there is no motivation to act.
