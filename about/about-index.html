About This Blog

This is a room the writing walks into. Not a résumé. Not a platform. Not a brand. Just a place where stories, arguments, and the occasional poem get put down in writing and left for whoever finds them.

The name is from Monty Python — the Black Knight, severed limbs and all, insisting the wound is minor. It seemed right for a blog that takes writing seriously and itself lightly.

The writing here covers whatever demands to be written about: short fiction, cultural commentary, travel with the wrong priorities, sports, faith, the strange experience of being alive in this particular moment. There is no theme. The writer is the theme.

A literary journal written from a quiet table in an old pub.

Posts are infrequent enough to mean something and frequent enough to stay honest. There are no ads, no sponsored content, no affiliate links. If you read something here, it's because someone thought it was worth writing — not because an algorithm decided you'd engage with it.

Influences

C.S. Lewis for the way he argued without condescension. Thomas Paine for the way he wrote like the stakes were real. Stephen King for the way he trusted the reader. The New Yorker circa 1960 for reasons that should be obvious.

About the Writer

Isaac Lindell is a Certified Financial Planner, a husband, and a father. He lives in Nashville. He reads more than he sleeps and writes more than he should given his schedule. This blog is his fault.
