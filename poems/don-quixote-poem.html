The Awaiting of Don Quixote

Poem

The sun-dewed days warm any soul anew,
And mustn't perish to the modern glow.
The cobblestone roads don a royal blue,
And happy siestas bound to and fro',
 'Tis a token for beauty and loves' care.
With artful designs from centuries past,
Masks on golden stilts with chapels aware,
Cling to the people, but are fading fast.
  Now madness, clawing up the red mountain
Obeying empires past this quaint kingdom,
Brings skulls with vultures to mimic fortune,
Praying no one begets Love to sing some.
   Centuries have passed, and she still stands small —
San Miguel de Allende, please fight tall.
