Sacrifice

Poem · From Goodness: A Layman's Interpretation

But what to a man is Sacrifice?
Why does such a treasure cost
The struggle within?
To each his own
Forever known, yet life's
Ease not alone. She is a thief,
Nature thee, of eyes to mule
Against creatures' rule over me
However cruel.

As time draws on
The dawn ticks on until dusk
Clicks on. We are laid bare,
Our body there, of which was slain,
From unknown pain of love
And from Caine.

Beneath a stone
Air grows thin to keep another
Without kin. Fellow men see
Angel Death's fee, the soul and time,
Judge the crime of others
The crime of mine.

Why may He
Stand for Good? In your place,
Evil stood. Yet a crown
Simmers down, daring beings
To claim such things of mercy
And things of kings.

A ruby shine
Stains thy shirt and writes
One's hurt. In vain shall it be
Credited unto thee? Night awakens
The Queen of memory
To be wise unto thee.

Dost thou mind
Thirst for fire and by sword
Quench desire? As a vulture must
The heart shall lust for more reward
With a fool's sword of lies
And swords of war.

But what to a man is Sacrifice?
